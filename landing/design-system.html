Design Language System

CardCompass Design System

Every design decision is intentional. This document explains not just what each token is, but why it exists — the research, psychology, and technical reasoning behind every choice in our 2026 cyberpunk fintech visual language.

Foundation

Design Principles

Three lenses through which every design decision must pass.

01

Trust Through Precision

This is a fintech app that reads bank statements. Every pixel must communicate competence, security, and technical authority. Sloppy design = zero trust.

02

Intelligence Made Visible

CardCompass runs a complex AI pipeline behind the scenes. The design must make this invisible complexity feel tangible — through neon glows, data visualizations, and terminal aesthetics that signal "smart technology at work."

03

Reward the User's Eyes

A rewards optimizer should itself feel rewarding to use. Micro-animations, satisfying transitions, and glowing accents create a "dopamine loop" that makes the app addictive — not just useful.

Color System

Chromatic Identity

A neon-on-midnight palette engineered for fintech trust, AI intelligence, and visual excitement.

Brand Palette

Neon Cyber Cyan
#00F5FF
Primary • CTAs • Active States
WHY: Cyan sits at the intersection of "trustworthy blue" (banks, finance) and "innovative green" (growth, money). We push it to neon intensity to signal that CardCompass isn't a traditional bank — it's a cutting-edge AI tool. The high luminance against dark backgrounds creates immediate visual hierarchy and draws the eye to primary actions. Mercury, Stripe, and Revolut all use high-saturation blues for the same reason — trust + modernity.
High-Tech Purple
#8B5CF6
Secondary • Gradients • AI Elements
WHY: Purple has centuries of association with luxury and intelligence. In modern tech contexts (Twitch, Figma, Stripe's gradient), it signals "smart" and "premium." We use it as a gradient partner for cyan, creating a spectrum that reads as "intelligent technology." The specific hue (HSL 264°, 90%, 66%) is calibrated to feel vivid without competing with cyan for attention.
Holographic Magenta
#FF007F
Accent • Hover States • Urgency
WHY: Hot pink/magenta creates urgency without the alarm of red. It's our "attention" color — used sparingly for hover states, badges, and elements that need to pop against the cyan-purple palette. The warmth (330° hue) balances the cool primary/secondary, preventing the interface from feeling cold or clinical. In cyberpunk aesthetics, magenta is the traditional complement to cyan.
Reward Gold
#FFD700
Rewards • Savings • Premium
WHY: Gold is universal shorthand for value and achievement. In a rewards optimizer, it's the color of money saved. We use it for earnings numbers, savings displays, milestone achievements, and premium tier indicators. The high chroma (51° hue, full saturation) ensures it reads as "real gold" rather than "dull yellow."

Surface Hierarchy

Five levels of darkness create spatial depth without 3D rendering. Each step is ~10-15 lightness units apart — enough to distinguish layers but close enough to feel cohesive.

Void#020810Page background — the deepest canvas
Base#050B18Primary content canvas
Raised#0C152BCards, panels, elevated content
Overlay#111D38Popovers, dropdowns, menus
Subtle#1E293BBorders, dividers, hairlines
WHY dark surfaces: Deep midnight tones do three things: (1) create the depth needed for neon colors to glow convincingly — neon on white looks wrong, (2) reduce eye strain for an app people use frequently, (3) signal sophistication — every premium fintech in 2026 (Mercury, Stripe, Robinhood) defaults to dark. The progression from #020810 to #1E293B follows a near-exponential lightness curve, giving each layer a distinct but subtle separation.
Typography

Type System

Three font families, each chosen for a specific role in the CardCompass voice.

Space Grotesk
Headings 700 / 800 weight -0.02em tracking
WHY: A geometric sans-serif with monospaced DNA — the letterforms have the precision of code but the warmth of a humanist font. This duality mirrors CardCompass itself: technical AI capability, human-friendly experience. The tight -0.02em letter-spacing at headline sizes creates the "confident, assured" typographic voice that premium fintech demands. Compare to Inter or Roboto, which are safer but lack personality.
Plus Jakarta Sans
Body Text 400 / 500 / 600 weight 1.6 line height
WHY: A modern, highly-readable humanist sans-serif designed for digital screens. Its slightly rounded terminals make body text feel approachable, counterbalancing the technical precision of Space Grotesk headings. At 400 weight with 1.6 line-height, it's optimized for long-form readability on dark backgrounds — crucial for an app that presents transaction data and financial insights.
JetBrains Mono
Code / Terminal 400 / 600 weight +0.05em tracking
WHY: The industry-standard monospace font for 2026, with increased x-height and carefully designed ligatures that make data pipelines and code blocks feel premium rather than raw. We use it for labels, badges, metadata, and the terminal UI — anywhere we want to invoke the "technical authority" aesthetic that reassures users the AI is doing serious work.

Type Scale — Major Third (1.25 ratio)

Each step is a 1.25× multiplier from the base 16px. This creates comfortable reading hierarchy without dramatic jumps — each size feels distinctly larger without overwhelming adjacent content.

Glass & Depth

Liquid Glass System

Frosted translucent panels create real depth and spatial hierarchy.

Glass Panel

This panel uses backdrop-filter: blur(20px) saturate(180%) with a semi-transparent background and 0.08 opacity border. The blur obscures detail while preserving color/light from the gradient orbs behind it.

bg: rgba(12, 21, 43, 0.55) border: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08) blur: 20px saturate: 180%
WHY glass effects: Frosted glass (backdrop-filter) creates literal visual depth — you can see content layered behind panels, which makes the interface feel three-dimensional and spatial without expensive 3D rendering. The 20px blur radius is calibrated: less than 15px and content is still readable (breaks the illusion); more than 30px and it's just an opaque color with extra GPU cost. The 180% saturation boost prevents the blur from looking "washed out." The 0.08 opacity border catches edge-light and defines the panel against the dark background — too thick and it looks cheap, too thin and the panel dissolves into nothing. Apple's "Liquid Glass" in 2025/2026 validated this aesthetic for mass-market appeal.
Animation

Motion System

Every animation serves a purpose: feedback, guidance, or delight. Nothing moves just for show.

Easing Curves

--ease-out-expo
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)

Natural deceleration — objects start fast and settle gently. Used for page entrances, element reveals, and navigation transitions. Mimics real-world physics (a ball rolling to a stop).

--ease-out-back
cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)

Slight overshoot — element goes past its target then springs back. Used for interactive feedback: buttons, toggles, popovers. Makes the interface feel "alive" and physically responsive.

--ease-spring
cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)

Smooth spring — fast start with a gentle, satisfying settle. Our default for most transitions. Feels precise and controlled, matching the "technical authority" design principle.

Duration Scale

--duration-micro150msHover, focus, micro-feedback
--duration-fast300msStandard transitions, state changes
--duration-normal500msElement entrances, reveals
--duration-slow800msHero animations, section reveals
--duration-slower1200msPage transitions, complex sequences
WHY these specific durations: Below 100ms, transitions feel instant (good for state but bad for showing change). 150ms is the minimum duration where the human eye registers "something moved." 300ms is the sweet spot for UI transitions — fast enough to not feel sluggish, slow enough to see the motion. Beyond 800ms, users start to feel the interface is "slow." Our scale stops at 1200ms, used only for dramatic hero sequences on first page load. Every duration above 150ms must use a non-linear easing curve — linear motion looks robotic and cheap.

Neon Glow Scale

Neon glow uses multi-layer box-shadows to mimic real light diffusion. A single shadow looks flat; stacked shadows at different blur radii create a tight bright core + a wide diffused halo — mimicking how neon tubes actually illuminate.

Accessibility

Motion & Inclusion

prefers-reduced-motion: reduce

~25% of users experience motion sensitivity. When this media query matches, we disable ALL non-essential animation — particle canvas, 3D card tilt, scroll reveals, magnetic cursor, counter animations, and terminal typing. Layout, color, and hover state changes are preserved. This is not optional — it's a fundamental design constraint that every feature must respect.

CSS-first animation strategy

All scroll-driven animations use native CSS animation-timeline: view(), which runs entirely off the main thread. This means zero JavaScript overhead, no IntersectionObserver polling, and guaranteed 60fps on capable browsers. For browsers without support, a lightweight JS fallback adds .in-view classes via IntersectionObserver. The JS layer only handles things CSS cannot: canvas particles, mouse tracking, and counter math.

Component

AI Panel (Inline Toggle)

A slide-down panel that reveals machine-readable product information without leaving the page.

WHY inline instead of redirect: The previous design navigated to llm.txt as a raw file. This broke the user's context — they lost their scroll position and had to use the browser's back button. The inline panel follows the progressive disclosure pattern: content is hidden until the user explicitly requests it, and they can dismiss it without losing their place. The toggle also uses aria-pressed for screen readers, making the open/closed state programmatically discoverable.

Toggle Behavior

  • • AI button in nav toggles .ai-panel.open
  • • Panel slides down from 72px (nav height) with max-height transition
  • • Content is fetched lazily on first open via fetch('llm.txt')
  • • Closeable via X button, Escape key, or re-clicking AI button
  • • position: fixed + z-index: 90 — overlays page content without reflow
WHY position: fixed: The panel overlays the page content rather than pushing it down. This avoids expensive layout reflows when toggling — the browser doesn't need to recalculate positions for every element below. It also preserves scroll position perfectly.

Pattern

Visual Hierarchy Patterns

How we guide the user's eye through each fold using size, contrast, and proximity.

01

Single Focal Point

The hero section uses ONE visual element (the compass illustration) instead of two competing images. Research shows that multiple focal points above the fold increase cognitive load and reduce conversion — the eye has no anchor.

02

Emphasized Center Card

In the Problem section, the center stat card (₹50K) uses transform: scale(1.08) and a gold border glow. This creates visual hierarchy across equal-content cards — size communicates importance without needing text labels.

03

CTA-Adjacent Social Proof

Social proof text ("183+ credit cards supported · 10+ Indian banks") is placed directly below the primary CTA button, not in a separate section. Research shows proximity between proof and action increases click-through by creating immediate validation.

04

Balanced Panel Visuals

Each How It Works panel has equal visual weight: Step 1 has an animated connection graphic, Steps 2–3 have app mockup screenshots. No panel is "text-only" — a visual vacuum breaks the narrative flow.
